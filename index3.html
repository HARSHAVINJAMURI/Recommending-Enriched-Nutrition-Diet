Health Conditions Related to Nutrition


Adverse reactions to foods

Lots of people think certain foods cause different symptoms. Although there isn't always strong scientific proof that foods are harmful, there is evidence showing that some reactions to foods can be bad. Allergies happen when the body's immune system reacts to a food, while intolerance happens when the body can't properly digest it.

Adverse reactions to natural food chemicals

Some foods contain natural chemicals. Certain ones like salicylates, amines, and monosodium glutamate (MSG) can cause reactions in sensitive individuals, leading to what's known as food intolerances.

Impact of diet on allergy risk

In recent decades, more and more people have been getting allergies. Scientists used to think cities caused most of them. But now, many studies suggest that what we eat could also play a big part in how likely we are to have allergies.

Malnutrition

Malnutrition happens when someone doesn't get enough energy to keep their body working properly. This includes growing, staying healthy, and moving around. It often leads to poor health because it can make you more likely to get sick or infected since your body's defense system doesn't work well.

Lactose intolerance

Lactose intolerance which means having trouble with foods that contain lactose like dairy, mostly impacts the stomach and digestion. It can also affect other parts of the body because of not getting enough nutrients due to this condition.

Hypercholesterolaemia

Hypercholesterolaemia means having too much cholesterol in your blood. If there's too much cholesterol, it can stick to the walls of your arteries. This can cause problems like heart disease and other serious health issues.

Metabolic syndrome

Metabolic syndrome happens when someone has a bunch of health problems at once, like trouble with insulin and being overweight. People with this syndrome are more likely to get heart disease and type 2 diabetes than those who don't have it.

Bulimia nervosa

Bulimia nervosa happens when someone eats a lot of food very quickly and then tries to get rid of the food by making themselves vomit or using laxatives. This behavior is usually to avoid gaining weight. It mostly affects women rather than men and often begins during teenage years.

Anorexia nervosa

Anorexia nervosa is a mental illness. People with this condition often feel very scared to eat because they have a problem with how they see their bodies. This can make them very thin and sick because they don't eat enough. Anorexia nervosa can be very dangerous and even deadly.
